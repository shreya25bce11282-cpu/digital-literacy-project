# Phishing – Cybercrime Case Study

## What is Phishing?
Phishing is a cyberattack where criminals impersonate trusted 
entities such as banks, universities, or companies through 
emails, SMS, or fake websites to steal login credentials, 
personal information, or financial details from unsuspecting 
victims. It is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, 
particularly targeting college students and young adults.

## How it Happens – Step by Step
1. The attacker sends an email appearing to be from a 
   legitimate source such as the victim's bank or university
2. The email creates urgency like: "Your account will be 
   suspended within 24 hours, click here to verify"
3. The link leads to a fake website that looks visually 
   identical to the real one
4. The victim, believing it to be genuine, enters their 
   username, password, or UPI PIN
5. The attacker captures the credentials instantly in 
   real time
6. The victim's account is drained or misused to 
   carry out further attacks on others

## Who is Targeted?
College students are commonly targeted through fake scholarship 
emails, free software offers, and fraudulent university fee 
payment links. First year students are especially vulnerable 
as they are new to managing their own bank accounts and UPI 
payments. Elderly people and small business owners are also 
frequent targets.

## Consequences
Victims suffer immediate financial loss, long term identity 
theft, and significant emotional distress. Academic and 
professional records can be compromised if institutional 
email accounts are accessed. In some cases the attacker 
uses the victim's identity to commit further crimes, which 
can lead to legal complications for the victim themselves 
even though they are the ones who were wronged.

## Scenario:

Riya, a first year engineering student at a college in Bhopal, 
received an email one morning that appeared to be from her 
university's finance department. The email stated that her 
semester fee payment had failed and that her enrollment would 
be cancelled within 24 hours unless she re verified her payment 
details immediately. Panicking, Riya clicked the link in the 
email which took her to a page that looked exactly like her 
university portal. She entered her student ID, password, and 
UPI PIN. Within minutes, Rs. 15,000 was debited from her 
account. When she called the university helpdesk, she learned 
that the email had never been sent by them. Riya had fallen 
victim to a phishing attack. She reported the incident to 
cybercrime.gov.in and called helpline 1930, but recovering 
the money proved difficult.
