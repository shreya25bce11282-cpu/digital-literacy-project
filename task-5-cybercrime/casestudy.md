# Phishing – Cybercrime Case Study

## What is Phishing?
Phishing is a cyberattack where criminals impersonate trusted entities such as banks, universities, or companies to steal login credentials or financial information from unsuspecting victims.

## How it Happens – Step by Step
1. The attacker sends an email appearing to be from a legitimate source, such as the victim's bank or university
2. The email creates urgency, for example, "Your account will be suspended within 24 hours, click here to verify"
3. The link leads to a fake website that looks identical to the real one
4. The victim enters their username, password, or UPI PIN
5. The attacker captures the credentials instantly
6. The victim's account is drained or misused for further attacks

## Who is Targeted?
College students are commonly targeted through fake scholarship emails, free software offers, and university fee payment links. 
Elderly people and anyone with a UPI linked bank account are also frequent targets.

## Consequences
Victims suffer financial loss, identity theft, and emotional distress. 
In some cases the attacker uses the victim's identity to commit further crimes, which can lead to legal complications for the victim themselves.
